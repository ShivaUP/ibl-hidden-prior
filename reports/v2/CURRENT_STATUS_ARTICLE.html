Title (working)

Comparing standard RNNs, predictive-coding credit assignment, and explicit Bayesian prior updating for hidden-block inference in the International Brain Laboratory visual decision task

Abstract (status summary)

Mice performing the International Brain Laboratory (IBL) contrast-discrimination task experience hidden blocks in which the prior probability of the rewarded stimulus side is biased (P(right) in {0.2, 0.5, 0.8}). Behavior indicates that animals update a latent block prior trial by trial. Here we ask which computational account best explains that updating when models are trained only on synthetic IBL-like sessions and then frozen for transfer to real sessions.

We implement four models sharing a common within-trial tick and channel schema: (i) a tanh RNN trained with backpropagation through time (tanh_bptt); (ii) an architecturally identical tanh RNN trained with local predictive-coding credit assignment (tanh_pc); (iii) a gated recurrent unit (GRU); and (iv) an explicit leaky Bayesian prior with a stimulus readout (bayes). Primary ranking uses held-out synthetic data under a history-only regime. Secondary evaluation transfers frozen weights to a shared real cohort of n=10 almost-perfect behavior sessions that also have Neuropixels coverage in MOs and/or ORBvl (vlOFC). Scoring is always against the correct stimulus side (not mouse choice). A neural variance-explained ladder compares model belief to region-level prior readouts.

On history-only ranking, the GRU currently leads both synthetic held-out accuracy and real transfer accuracy, with a large history gap (differential zero-evidence preference between 0.8 and 0.2 blocks). The predictive-coding tanh is weaker when it must infer the prior from history alone, but leads under an oracle full-information regime—consistent with limited learned history use rather than a general inability to use prior information. On the shared n=10 neural cohort, session-mean ve_linear_recal also ranks GRU first in both MOs and vlOFC; a matched GRU vs tanh_bptt advantage survives Holm correction in MOs but not in vlOFC.

1. Introduction / scientific question

1.1 Biological setting

The IBL decision task requires a left/right choice based on a visual grating of variable contrast. Across blocks, the experimenter-imposed prior over the rewarded side switches among 0.2, 0.5, and 0.8 without an explicit cue. Mice show psychometric shifts and switch-centered adaptation consistent with updating a latent prior.

1.2 Computational question

Which model class best explains trial-by-trial updating of that hidden prior?

  • Class A — Standard task-trained RNN (tanh_bptt; also GRU as a gated variant)
  • Class B — Predictive-coding / local credit-assignment training of the same tanh architecture (tanh_pc)
  • Class C — Explicit online inference (bayes: leaky subjective prior q_t)

Note: a dedicated meta-RL RNN is parked in the v2 freeze and is not trained in the current artifact set. The implemented comparison therefore uses four model IDs spanning the three scientific classes above (standard RNN = tanh_bptt + GRU; PC = tanh_pc; explicit inference = bayes).

1.3 Evaluation philosophy (locked)

  • Train ONLY on synthetic IBL-like sessions (empirical generator statistics).
  • Rank primarily on held-out synthetic data (same generator, unseen sessions).
  • Transfer FROZEN weights to real mouse sessions (no mouse fine-tuning).
  • Separate behavioral evaluation from neural alignment.
  • Prefer behavior-matched neural comparisons when claiming neural advantage.

2.2 Behavioral quality control (almost-perfect gates)

Implemented in src/data/qc.py / session QC reports (reports/neural/neural_roi_qc_scores.json for the neural-capable pool).

Trial-level rules (1–4): finite stimOn, goCue, response, feedback times; monotonic ordering stim ≤ go ≤ response ≤ feedback; reaction time RT = response − goCue (stimOn fallback) > 0; choice in {−1, +1}.

RT trim: keep trials within the [1, 99] percentile of RT within session.

Session-level gates (all required): ≥ 400 choice trials; probabilityLeft values ⊆ {0.2, 0.5, 0.8}; presence of left-bias, right-bias, AND unbiased blocks; ≥ 95% of choice trials pass rules 1–4; ≥ 90% remain after RT percentile trim.

All 25 parent-pool sessions and all 10 selected sessions satisfy passes_session == True under these gates.

2.3 Trial encoding: shared tick / channel schema

Documentation: docs/TICK_AND_CHANNELS.md , configs/synthetic_v2.yaml.

Timebase: discrete ticks of 100 ms. Current schedule uses 9 ticks per trial with causal order baseline → stimulus → go → response → feedback. Choice loss / readout only on the response-phase tick(s).

Input channels (7-dimensional observation vector): visual_right, visual_left, go_cue, action_left, action_right, rewarded, not_rewarded. Visual coding is side × magnitude (not binary high/low). Contrast levels follow IBL empirical support {0, 0.0625, 0.125, 0.25, 0.5, 1.0}.

Synthetic sensory noise: σ = 0.15 on visual channels during synth training/eval. Real transfer: sensory noise OFF.

2.4 Synthetic task generator

Generator statistics are fit from empirical behavior (behavior-core legacy pin) so that synthetic sessions match real block-length, transition, contrast, and trial-count distributions without replaying specific real eid sequences. Block priors ∈ {0.2, 0.5, 0.8}. Sessions are long (~900 trials for BPTT/GRU/Bayes training schedules). This design keeps real transfer meaningful: models never see the evaluation mice during training.

2.5 Training and scoring targets

Choice target (train and eval): CORRECT STIMULUS SIDE only — not mouse choice and not mouse P(right). On real rollouts, the mouse's action and reward appear as history INPUTS on feedback ticks, but do not define the supervised label.

Training feedback (synth): teacher-forced action / outcome with training_feedback_error_rate = 0.2. Synth held-out eval: closed-loop (model's own action feeds subsequent history). Real transfer: frozen weights; no fine-tuning on mice.

2.6 Evaluation regimes

Three regimes share the same models and schema (src/models_v2/rollout.py):

  • history_only (PRIMARY) — Model must infer the block prior from history / feedback alone. This is the scientifically central condition for latent prior updating.
  • full_information (CONTROL / UPPER REFERENCE) — At evaluation time, an oracle log-odds bias from the true block prior is injected (fi_oracle_logit_gain = 2.5). Tests whether a model can USE prior information when it is provided.
  • fixed_prior (CONTROL) — Restrict to trials/blocks with P(right) ≈ 0.5 (no 0.2/0.8 switches). History gap is undefined.

Shared implementation notes: NumPy reference implementations under src/models_v2/; RNN hidden size = 48; choice head = 2-way logits at response tick with cross-entropy vs correct side; checkpoints at artifacts/v2/models/{model_id}/.

2.8 Behavioral metrics

  • Accuracy: argmax choice == correct stimulus side.
  • Cross-entropy (CE) / choice NLL vs correct side.
  • History gap: counterfactual zero-evidence probe (contrast = 0); history_gap = mean P(right | true 0.8 block) − mean P(right | true 0.2 block). Near 0 ⇒ little differential prior use; large positive ⇒ strong block-tuned bias.
  • Settled-block calibration: trials ≥ 15 after last switch; compare zero-evidence preference to true prior.
  • Switch-centered analysis: window −20 … +30 trials relative to a block prior change; directions 0.2→0.8 and 0.8→0.2.
  • Psychometric-by-block: choice probability vs contrast, stratified by true block prior.

Synth regime evaluation size: typically 32 held-out synthetic sessions × 929 trials (history/full); fixed_prior filters to P≈0.5. Real regime (shared cohort): n_sessions = 10; n_trials_pooled = 6613 (history_only / full_information); fixed_prior = 887 trials.

2.9 Subjective / model prior probes

For all models, the operational belief used in figures and neural alignment is the counterfactual zero-evidence choice probability (or explicit q_t for Bayes), obtained by holding sensory contrast at 0 while keeping recurrent / prior state from the real history. This isolates latent prior influence from sensory evidence.

2.10 Neural analysis methods

Regions in scope: MOs and vlOFC (ORBvl). Spike features: peri-stimulus spike counts in window NeuralWindow(t_start=−0.1 s, t_end=0.3 s) aligned to stimOn_times.

Neural prior readout n_t: (1) estimate mouse subjective prior axis from behavior (mouse_prior_hat); (2) cross-validated Ridge regression spikes → mouse_prior_hat; (3) predicted values = neural prior readout n_t.

Model belief q_t: synth-trained model, frozen, history_only, on the same trials, using the zero-evidence / explicit prior probe.

Primary alignment metric: ve_linear_recal = variance explained of n_t by a linearly recalibrated q_t, then mean across sessions.

Behavior matching before neural ranking: ε-ball on real history_only choice CE (ε = 0.05). On the n=10 cohort all four models match. Survival test: session bootstrap of matched best-vs-second VE advantage with Holm correction across regions (α = 0.05). Scripts: 13_eval_neural_pilot.py → 14_eval_neural_matched.py → 15_make_neural_figures.py.

4. Discussion (current interpretation)

4.1 What is supported now

  • A reproducible synth→real pipeline exists with a shared tick schema and correct-side-only scoring.
  • Under the primary history-only regime, a gated standard RNN (GRU) currently best explains both synthetic held-out behavior and frozen real transfer, with a large history gap indicating strong latent prior use.
  • An architecturally matched PC-trained tanh does not match BPTT/GRU on history inference, but can outperform when the prior is supplied—suggesting a specific deficit in learned history-based prior updating under the present PC training schedule, not a general inability to exploit priors.
  • Explicit Bayes updates in the expected direction but with a smaller history gap than the best RNNs, consistent with a lower-capacity / more constrained latent state.

4.2 What is not yet safe to claim

  • A large or region-general neural advantage of GRU over tanh_bptt (MOs survival is small ΔVE; vlOFC does not survive Holm on n=6).
  • That PC is intrinsically worse than BPTT for this task (training length / hyperparameters differ; intentional but not exhaustively ablated).
  • Mouse-choice prediction as a primary claim (current scoring is correct side).
  • Meta-RL comparisons (parked).

4.3 Behavior-matched neural logic

Even if one model aligns better to neural prior readouts, that advantage is only scientifically decisive if models are matched on behavior. On the n=10 shared cohort, all four models pass the CE ε=0.05 ball, so the neural ranking (GRU > tanh_bptt > bayes > tanh_pc) is not an artifact of excluding PC.

5. Limitations and open risks

  • vlOFC coverage is only 6/10 sessions; survival underpowered relative to MOs.
  • GRU vs tanh_bptt neural ΔVE is small even where it survives (MOs).
  • tanh_pc trained with shorter sessions (240 vs 929 trials) for stability.
  • Bayes state capacity << hidden-48 RNNs (fairness documented, not equalized).
  • Real feedback distribution shift: teacher-forced synth training vs mouse action/reward inputs at transfer.
  • RT modeling / RT heads are out of v2 primary scope (RT used for QC only).
  • Full-information CE values are not directly comparable to history_only CE because the oracle logit bias saturates probabilities; use accuracy + gap.
  • Meta-RL and Bayes+CA twins remain parked.
